Sequences in the cloister

Set up a stage space for a period of three days to discover the Royal Abbey in a new light and mark its opening on the city.

This project consists in creating a space in the cloister around three sequences in order to give rhythm to the course. The first sequence : a photographic exhibition space to highlight the architecture of the abbey. Then, a catering space to offer a break to visitors. Finally, a space of representations where an association of artistic action will come to do representations : theater, circus, singing...

  • Category: Set design
  • Country: France, Nouvelle Aquitaine
  • Location: Royal Abbey of Saint-Jean d'Angely

Steps in the cloister

Sequence 01 : The exibition space is a moment of discovery thanks to a photographic exhibition on the royal abbey.

Sequence 02 : The catering space is a moment of pause thanks to a refreshment bar allowing visitors to eat or drink.

Sequence 03 : The space of representations is a moment of relaxation thanks to various spectacles which will take place all the afternoon.

A sequence in the abbey

Set up an outdoor course through several sequences highlighting the architectural and historical heritage of the Royal Abbey of Saint-Jean d'Angely. This abbey is classified as an historic monument since 1985.

This project consists in creating a pedagogical path to discover the monastic life that has disappeared for several years. The scenographic journey refers to the daily life of a monk : his practices, his way of life... The itinerary also transcribes the atmosphere and the history of each place thanks to the light once the night comes.

  • Category: Set design
  • Country: France, Nouvelle-Aquitaine
  • Location: Royal Abbey of Saint-Jean d'Angely

Steps : 1) Chapel 2) Well 3) Cloister 4) Court of honor 5) Court of the Angevins

Second breath

Design a luxury experience embodied by a space whose specifications were randomly constituted. The space was described according to five parameters: In the water (context) ; Sportsmen (target) ; 100m2 (area) ; Short-lived (biased) ; Street lamp (object)

The main intention is to offer visitors a sporting experience that questions the apprehension of effort through water and sublimates the movement of the body through the light. The goal is to poetize the practice of sport and to reveal the power of the sensations that it can give us. It is not a narcissistic body but a proven body, allowing us to access our sensations and create intimacy with oneself.

  • Category: Set design
  • Country: France, Nancy
  • Location: Stanislas place

The experience is an evolutionary course plunged into darkness, where the difficulty grows more and more during the walk. From the outside, the stratified structure shows this movement in relation to sport and water, like an oscillation.

When the temperature and humidity of the room increase, a soundtrack amplifies the feeling of an effort by giving a rhythm faster and faster to the course. The senses of visitors are stimulated and water becomes a tool to access its sensations: perspiration (moisture), dehydration (dry air), vision that is cloudy (steam) ... These tools condition and shape the feeling of an intense effort.

The visitors participate in several, with family or friends, and are equipped with a light combination where the light draws a line from the shoulder to the ankle to highlight the movement of the joints. In several strategic locations in space, a photographic device captures their movement using the Light-Painting process. Photos in light-painting are collected and given as a souvenir at the end of the course.

A nest for a weaver

The Hall of Rouvray, an industrial building built in 1936, is available to welcome craftsmen in the crafts sector. The Hall is divided into seven lots : each lot must allow craftsmen to have both a work space and a living space.

The project adapts to the craftsman who lives there. Here weaving is shown in all its form (canvas, mesh) to ensure several functions. For the workshop space of 38m2, the canvas is used to have transparency from the main hallway of the Hall and see the weaver working. For the living space of 21,6m2, the mesh allows the weaver to have an intimate space, like a nest that he himself would weave.

  • Category: Interior design
  • Country: France, Paris
  • Location: Hall of Rouvray

Make me dancing

Create a luxury space of 30m2 and respond to needs of a specific and particular client.

Create a microarchitecture to welcome Marc's passion. He would enjoy the luxury of a private space devoted entirely to dance. A modular, deployable, light structure that seems to accompany the dancer in his movements. Micro-architecture seeks to get closer to the dimensions of the human body, to economic and environmental realities. This imaginary mode of construction makes it possible to approach a plastic and social experiment.

  • Category: Landscaping
  • Country: England, Dover
  • Location: White cliffs of Dover

A hermitage far from everything

The white cliffs of Dover are located in England in Kent County. High of 110 meters, they offer a remarkable panorama on the Channel.

  • Program: Leisure Shelter for one person.
  • Mission: Sports experience and rest area.
  • Surfaces: Ground floor : 16m2 / First floor : 14m2

The ground floor allows the user to start warming the body, to stretch, before dancing. The first floor is the living place of the dancer. It is its place of rest and privacy where a bay window gives a remarkable view of the sea.

Axonometry of the structure on the cliff. It opens on the panorama so that Marc disconnects from his daily life.

Poles are the joints of micro-architecture. Thanks to them, Marc can come to dance in this frame and to appropriate the space. The structure thus becomes Marc's dance partner.

Settling at the seaside offers a peaceful, quiet and silent setting. Only the sounds and movements produced by the waves enliven the landscape. Infinite landscape, which suggests a great freedom: escape, fugue, escapade, lightness, reverie, imagination, distraction...

Wired models made following the study of the body movements of the dancer. Two main positions for architecture: one closed and one open to create an interaction with the user.

Progressive signage

For Michel Lussault, a « hyperplace » is a space of shared experiences where one finds there familiarity with others, a place of mixity where on is there « for the same thing ». Michel Pena, landscaper, says « So, we must reinvent the outside (...) because it is a rich experience, amazing, fulfilling, an incredible adventure ».

The goal is to create a meeting space between these two houses but also a space of culture. This is how the project is based on a plant signage that evolves to attract students to come and encourage them to stay on the plot.

  • Category: Landscaping
  • Country: France, Paris
  • Location: International university campus

Meeting plot - location

Three spaces in one

1) Meeting place : sharing and exchange 2) Liaison space : movement 3) Farming space : olfactory and discovery 4) Micro-architecture used as a store for storing tools 5) Furniture used as signage to encourage students to water the plants in the plot : Plant watered = heavy planter and placed on the ground / Gradually, the plant will grow and drink water / Plant that needs to be watered = hanging planter

An animated plot

This evolution allows to link the meeting and the farming space. Students are first attracted by the furniture used as signage. They can come and water the plants in the culture area. Then, the meeting space welcomes them and offers them a moment of relaxation and exchange.

Space by micro-vegetation

Workshop with a 3D printer brand.

From the exhibition « Print in the world» at the Pompidou Center in 2017, the idea was to work on evolution and scales that allows to go from the micro to the macro. Thus, we started from a vegetal landscape to finish at a total space. To do this, we took the aspects of a plant module that we zoomed in three times to achieve redraw and rework the ribs. Once completed, we could notice, through the projected shadow, that there was a multitude of networks that, on a large scale, created spaces. Thus, from the micro plant, we get a space.

  • Category: Art

Study on different leaves and their veins

Scale game from a plant module zoomed in three stages : 1) Main veins of the leaf visible to the naked eye (macroscopic) 2) Small veins visible to the naked eye 3) Microscopic ribs

Modeling the different types of ribs according to their stage in order to print them in 3D

Plant modules

From a macroscopic plant module, a shadow appears in the form of networks creating a total space, at the macroscopic level.
